> Three useful substitutions:
> $$ x = a \tan \theta, x = a \sin \theta, x = a \sec \theta $$
> These will be handy when integration something with the form $x^2 + a^2, a^2 - x^2, x^2 - a^2$.

Why use $x = a \tan \theta$ for $x^2 + a^2$?
