Idea:
$\frac{d}{dx} (\sin(x^3)) = \cos(x^3) \cdot 3x^2$,
so it follows that
$\int 3x^2 \cos(x^3) \, dx = \sin(x^3) + C$.

### Example

> $\int x^2 \cos(x^3) \, dx$

$= \int \frac{1}{3} \cdot 3x^2 \cos(x^3) \, dx$
$= \frac{1}{3} \int 3x^2 \cos(x^3) \, dx$
$= \frac{1}{3} \sin(x^3)$
